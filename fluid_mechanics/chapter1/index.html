    Chapter 1: Introduction to Fluid Mechanics¶

    Definition of Fluid Mechanics

    Fluid Mechanics is the branch of physics that studies the behavior of fluids (liquids and gases) at rest (fluid statics) and in motion (fluid dynamics).

    1. Continuum Hypothesis¶

    Continuum Hypothesis

    The Continuum Hypothesis assumes that fluid properties like density, pressure, and velocity are continuously distributed in space. This allows the use of differential calculus. The fluid is treated as a continuous medium, ignoring molecular structure.

    Note: This assumption is valid when the characteristic length scale is much larger than the molecular mean free path.

    2. Classification of Fluids and Flow Types¶

    Classification of Fluids

    • Ideal Fluid: No viscosity, incompressible
    • Real Fluid: Viscous, may be compressible
    • Newtonian Fluid: Shear stress is linearly proportional to the rate of strain
    • Non-Newtonian Fluid: Nonlinear stress-strain relationship

    Types of Fluid Flow

    • Steady vs Unsteady: Time-independent vs time-dependent
    • Uniform vs Non-uniform: Same properties across a section vs varying spatially
    • Laminar vs Turbulent: Smooth orderly flow vs chaotic flow
    • Compressible vs Incompressible: Varying vs constant density

    3. Basic Fluid Properties¶

    Density (\( \rho \))

    Density is the mass per unit volume:

    \[ \rho = \frac{m}{V} \]

    Typical value: For water at 4°C, \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m³}\)

    Viscosity (\( \mu, \nu \))

    • Dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) relates shear stress to velocity gradient:
    \[ \tau = \mu \frac{du}{dy} \]
    • Kinematic viscosity \(\nu = \mu / \rho\)

    Surface Tension (\( \sigma \))

    \[ \sigma = \frac{F}{L} \]

    Water at 20°C: \(\sigma \approx 0.072 \, \text{N/m}\)

    Compressibility (\( \beta \))

    \[ \beta = -\frac{1}{V} \left( \frac{\partial V}{\partial P} \right)_T \]

    Pressure (\( P \))

    \[ P = \frac{F}{A} \]

    4. Introduction to Fluid Statics and Dynamics¶

    Fluid Statics and Dynamics

    • Fluid Statics: Fluids at rest. Focuses on pressure distribution, buoyancy, forces on submerged bodies.
    • Fluid Dynamics: Fluids in motion. Focuses on velocity fields, flow patterns, kinematics, and conservation laws.

    Key Distinctions Between Statics and Dynamics

    • Statics: \(\vec{v} = 0\), only pressure and body forces.
    • Dynamics: \(\vec{v} \neq 0\), velocity, acceleration, and shear stress are important.
    • Flow visualization:
      • Streamlines: Lines tangent to velocity vectors
      • Pathlines: Trajectories of individual particles

    5. Example Problems and Solutions¶

    Example 1: Surface Tension Force Around a Ring¶

    Problem: Calculate surface tension force around a circular ring of radius \(r = 2 \, \text{cm}\), if \(\sigma = 0.072 \, \text{N/m}\)

    Solution:

    \[ L = 2 \pi r = 2 \pi (0.02) = 0.1257 \, \text{m} $$ $$ F = \sigma \cdot L = 0.072 \cdot 0.1257 = 0.00905 \, \text{N} \]

    Answer: \(F = 9.05 \, \text{mN}\)

    Example 2: Shear Stress in a Newtonian Fluid¶

    Problem: A Newtonian fluid has \(\mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\), velocity gradient \(du/dy = 100 \, \text{s}^{-1}\)

    Solution:

    \[ \tau = \mu \frac{du}{dy} = 0.001 \cdot 100 = 0.1 \, \text{Pa} \]

    Answer: \(\tau = 0.1 \, \text{Pa}\)
